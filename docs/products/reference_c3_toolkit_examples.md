# C3 Toolkit: Examples Library

**When you know the strategy but need concrete examples**

---

## How to Use This Toolkit

This is a reference library. You do not need to read it all at once.

Use it when:
- You recognize a spending pattern but are not sure which C3 strategy applies
- You have chosen a C3 path (CUT, CANCEL, COMBINE) but need specific examples
- You are stuck and need ideas for alternatives

Skim the category that matches your situation. Pick one example that feels realistic. Try it.

---

## CUT Examples (Reduce Frequency)

### Subscriptions & Services
- Premium tier → Basic tier (keep the service, downgrade the level)
- Annual plan → Monthly plan (easier to cancel if needs change)
- Auto-renew → Manual renew (forces you to decide each time)
- Multiple platforms in same category → Keep favorite only
- Daily email digest → Weekly digest

### Food & Dining
- Daily coffee runs → 2-3 times per week
- Takeout 4-5 times/month → 1-2 times/month
- Meal kits every week → Every other week
- Lunch out daily → Pack lunch 3 days/week, buy 2 days
- Drive-through breakfast → Weekends only

### Shopping & Impulse Buys
- Same-day purchasing → 24-hour wait rule
- Browsing when bored → 15-minute timer, then close tabs
- Click-to-buy → Add to cart, wait 48 hours
- New items weekly → New items monthly
- Trending purchases → Wait until trend settles to see if you still want it

### Business Tools & Courses
- New course every month → Must finish current course first
- Buying every bundle → One bundle per quarter max
- Premium tools across multiple categories → Consolidate to essentials
- Attend every paid workshop → Choose 2-3 per year that align with current goals
- Software trials → Finish evaluating one before starting another

### Entertainment & Hobbies
- New books before finishing current stack → Finish 3 books before buying more
- Streaming new releases immediately → Wait until initial hype settles
- Buying supplies for every idea → Use what you have first
- Upgrading gear annually → Every 2-3 years unless broken
- Event tickets on impulse → Check calendar and budget first

### Personal Care & Wellness
- Salon visits every 4 weeks → Every 6-8 weeks
- New skincare products monthly → Finish current products first
- Supplements added frequently → Research + wait 30 days before adding new ones
- Workout classes unlimited → Set monthly class limit
- Spa treatments monthly → Quarterly

### Household & Convenience
- Cleaning service weekly → Bi-weekly
- Grocery delivery every order → In-store pickup (usually cheaper)
- Amazon Prime same-day → Standard shipping
- Convenience store runs → Weekly grocery trip planning
- Professional services → DIY when reasonable

## CANCEL Examples (Stop Entirely or Downgrade)

### Subscriptions & Services
- Unused gym membership → Canceled (use YouTube workouts, outdoor exercise)
- Three streaming services → Keep one, cancel two
- Magazine subscriptions not being read → Canceled
- Software with overlapping features → Keep one, cancel duplicates
- Email newsletters creating FOMO → Unsubscribed
- Cloud storage at multiple providers → Consolidate to one
- Premium music service → Free tier with ads
- Meal kit service → Canceled (grocery shop + recipe sites)

### Business Tools
- Project management tool not being used → Canceled (use free alternative or spreadsheet)
- Stock photo subscription → Canceled (use free stock photo sites)
- Premium domain hosting → Basic plan
- SEO tools with duplicate features → Keep one comprehensive tool
- Multiple email marketing platforms → Choose one
- Webinar platform rarely used → Pay per use instead of monthly
- Design software subscription → One-time purchase or free alternative

### Digital Services
- VPN service not used → Canceled
- Password manager premium → Free tier
- Website builder subscription → Canceled (move to self-hosted or free platform)
- Online courses not being completed → Canceled, focus on free resources
- App subscriptions for features you do not use → Free version or uninstall
- Gaming subscriptions → Canceled (buy games you will actually play)

### Memberships
- Professional association not providing value → Canceled
- Costco/Sam's Club membership unused → Canceled (shop regular grocery)
- Wine/book/subscription boxes → Canceled
- Premium loyalty programs → Free tier
- Country club or recreational memberships → Canceled if not using regularly

### Financial Services
- Bank accounts with monthly fees → Switch to free checking
- Credit monitoring service → Free alternatives exist
- Investment app premium features → Use basic free version
- Overdraft protection fees → Canceled (set up alerts instead)

### Household & Auto
- Lawn service → DIY or neighbor kid
- Car wash membership → Wash at home
- Premium cable package → Streaming only or basic cable
- Landline phone → Canceled (mobile only)
- Extended warranties → Canceled (most are not worth it)

## COMBINE Examples (Consolidate or Share)

### Subscriptions & Streaming
- Solo Netflix plan → Family plan split with household
- Multiple music services → One family plan
- Individual cloud storage → Shared family storage plan
- Separate app subscriptions → Family sharing where available
- Duplicate streaming services → One household account

### Shopping & Bulk Buying
- Individual grocery trips → Combined weekly trip with household
- Buying new every time → Borrow from friend, family, or tool library first
- Solo bulk club membership → Split with neighbor or family member
- Individual purchases → Group buy with friends (split cost of bulk items)
- New supplies → Use what you already have

### Business & Professional Tools
- Three separate tools for email/CRM/scheduling → One all-in-one platform
- Solo software licenses → Team plan split with colleagues
- Individual courses → Group coaching program (more support, shared cost)
- Separate hosting for multiple sites → Consolidated hosting plan
- Multiple design tools → One comprehensive tool (Canva, Adobe, etc.)

### Transportation & Services
- Individual rides → Carpool
- Solo gym membership → Workout with friend at their gym as guest
- Separate music lessons for kids → Group class
- Individual meal prep → Meal swap with neighbor
- Expensive branded items → Generic store brand

### Household & Family
- Individual phone plans → Family plan
- Separate yard tools for each neighbor → Tool sharing group
- Buying everything new for kids → Hand-me-downs, swaps, consignment
- Expensive kids activities → Community center or free alternatives
- Pet sitting services → Neighbor pet swap

### Digital & Online
- Multiple website builders → One platform for all sites
- Separate email accounts with paid storage → Consolidated free accounts
- Individual online courses → Library access or free alternatives
- Paid research tools → University library access (if available)
- Premium news sites → One subscription, share articles with household

### Entertainment & Recreation
- Individual event tickets → Group rates
- Solo hobbies with expensive supplies → Join a club that provides materials
- Buying books new → Library, Little Free Library, book swaps
- Individual streaming accounts → Share account with trusted person
- Expensive outings → Free community events, nature activities

## Category-Specific Examples

### For Singles
- **CUT**: Daily coffee shop → 2x per week, make coffee at home other days
- **CANCEL**: Unused dating app premium → Free version
- **COMBINE**: Solo Netflix → Share family plan with sibling

### For Couples
- **CUT**: Eating out 4x/week → 1x/week date night
- **CANCEL**: Duplicate streaming services → Keep household favorite
- **COMBINE**: Two separate gym memberships → One family membership

### For Families
- **CUT**: Kids activities from 5/week → 2-3/week
- **CANCEL**: Toys subscription box → Use what you have, library, swaps
- **COMBINE**: Separate phone plans → Family plan with 4-5 lines

### For Pet Parents
- **CUT**: Specialty pet treats weekly → Monthly
- **CANCEL**: Pet subscription boxes → Buy only what you need
- **COMBINE**: Expensive vet visits → Pet insurance (if worth it) or wellness plan

### For Business Owners
- **CUT**: Networking events every week → Once per month
- **CANCEL**: Tools with 80% unused features → Simpler alternative
- **COMBINE**: Five marketing tools → One integrated platform

## How to Choose Which C3 Strategy

**Ask these questions:**

1. **Do I use this regularly?**
   - Yes, and it adds value → Maybe CUT frequency or cost
   - Yes, but basic version would work → CANCEL premium, downgrade to basic
   - No, barely use it → CANCEL entirely
   - Someone else could use it too → COMBINE/share

2. **Is this a solo cost that could be shared?**
   - Yes → COMBINE (family plan, group buy, cost split)
   - No → Move to next question

3. **Do I have duplicates of this?**
   - Yes → COMBINE into one tool/service/platform
   - No → Move to next question

4. **Is the frequency out of alignment?**
   - Yes, happens too often → CUT to lower frequency
   - No, frequency is fine → Keep as is

5. **Is there a cheaper alternative that works just as well?**
   - Yes → COMBINE (replace expensive with cheaper)
   - No → Keep as is

## Examples by Dollar Amount

### Under $10/month
- **CUT**: Reduce frequency (coffee, snacks, small treats)
- **CANCEL**: Micro-subscriptions you forget about, app upgrades
- **COMBINE**: Generic vs. brand name items

### $10-$30/month
- **CUT**: Downgrade tier (music, cloud storage)
- **CANCEL**: Unused subscriptions (magazines, apps, memberships)
- **COMBINE**: Share family plans (streaming, storage)

### $30-$100/month
- **CUT**: Reduce service frequency (salon, meal kits, classes)
- **CANCEL**: Premium tiers you do not use fully
- **COMBINE**: Multiple tools into one platform

### $100+/month
- **CUT**: Reduce scope (fewer add-ons, lower tier)
- **CANCEL**: Services not aligned with current goals
- **COMBINE**: Consolidate vendors, negotiate group rates

## When None of These Fit

Not every expense will fit CUT, CANCEL, or COMBINE. That is fine.

Some expenses are:
- Essential and appropriately priced
- Already optimized
- Worth the cost for the value they provide
- One-time necessities

The C3 Framework is not about forcing every purchase into a category. It is about building the muscle of asking the question.

If you ask "Could I CUT, CANCEL, or COMBINE this?" and the answer is "No, this is aligned as is," then you move on.

The goal is awareness, not perfection.
