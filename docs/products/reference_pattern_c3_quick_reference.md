# Chasing Patterns + C3 Strategies: Quick Reference

**One-page cheat sheet for pattern recognition and action**

---

## How to Use This Reference

1. Notice a spending urge or pattern
2. Identify which Chasing Pattern (if any) fits
3. Check the recommended C3 strategy
4. Choose one example action to try

**Pattern names are optional.** If they do not resonate, skip to the C3 column and use what works.

---

## The Matrix

| **Pattern** | **What It Looks Like** | **Recommended C3** | **Example Action** |
|-------------|------------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Chasing Rainbows** | Buying the next fix, solution, or resource that promises to solve the problem or unlock success | **CANCEL** | Stop buying courses/templates for 30 days. Work with what you already own. |
| **Chasing Unicorns** | Spending to become the version of yourself you think you should be, often triggered by comparison | **CUT or CANCEL** | Unfollow accounts that make you feel "less than." Cancel aesthetic upgrades. Reduce comparison shopping. |
| **Chasing Ambrosia** | Buying to change how you feel, numb discomfort, or soothe overwhelm (emotional escape) | **CUT** | No purchases when feeling exhausted or flooded. Build a non-spending comfort menu first. Reduce frequency of emotional purchases. |
| **Chasing Stardust** | Spending tied to appearance, polish, or the belief that something external will make you look more legitimate | **CANCEL or COMBINE** | Stop buying "prettier" versions of what you have. Use what you have as is. Consolidate tools to one per category. |
| **Chasing Sunshine** | Small frequent purchases that add up, often framed as "I deserve this" or "it's just..." | **CUT** | Limit treats to 1-2 times per week instead of daily. Set a spending cap for indulgences (e.g., treats under $10 instead of $20). |
| **Chasing Moonbeams** | Buying for an imagined future self or life that does not exist yet (fantasy purchases) | **CANCEL or COMBINE** | No purchases tied to "when I..." thinking. Keep a "someday" list but do not buy. Consolidate all fantasy wishlists into one place. |

---

## Quick Decision Guide

**When you notice a spending urge, ask:**

1. **Is this a pattern I recognize?**
   - Yes → Check the matrix above
   - No → Move to question 2

2. **Could I CUT, CANCEL, or COMBINE this?**
   - **CUT** if you want to keep it but reduce frequency
   - **CANCEL** if you are not using it or a cheaper version would work
   - **COMBINE** if you could share costs or consolidate services

3. **What is the smallest action I could take right now?**
   - Add a 24-hour wait rule
   - Unsubscribe from one email list
   - Text an accountability person
   - Write in the "Almost Bought It" log

## C3 Strategies at a Glance

| **C3 Path** | **What It Means** | **Best For** |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **CUT** (Reduce) | Keep it but reduce how often it happens | Habits that serve you in moderation but cause problems at high frequency |
| **CANCEL** (Stop or Downgrade) | Stop entirely OR downgrade to lower tier | Services you are not using, duplicate tools, upgrades that do not deliver enough value |
| **COMBINE** (Consolidate or Share) | Share costs, consolidate services, or replace with cheaper alternative | Duplicate services, solo costs that could be shared, expensive items with cheaper alternatives |

## Pattern Interventions (Without C3)

If you recognize a pattern but are not sure which C3 strategy fits, try the direct intervention:

- **Rainbows**: Work with what exists. Stop collecting.
- **Unicorns**: You already are who you are. Choose enough, not perfect.
- **Ambrosia**: Meet the need directly instead of outsourcing it to money.
- **Stardust**: Use what you have exactly as it is.
- **Sunshine**: Change the state, not the cart.
- **Moonbeams**: Let desire exist without answering it immediately.

## When to Use This Reference

- **During the challenge**: When you feel a spending urge or notice a familiar pattern
- **At weekly check-ins**: To see which patterns showed up most frequently
- **After a slip**: To identify which C3 strategy would prevent this pattern next time
- **Planning for next round**: To refine your C3 commitments based on what you learned
