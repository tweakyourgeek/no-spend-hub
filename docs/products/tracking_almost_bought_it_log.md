# "Almost Bought It" Log

**Track the purchases you resisted**

---

## Why This Log Matters

Most no-spend tracking focuses on what you bought. This log focuses on what you did not buy.

Every time you feel the urge to purchase something and choose not to, that is a win. It means you noticed the pattern. You paused. You made a different choice.

This log captures those moments.

Over time, you will see:
- Which patterns trigger spending urges most frequently
- Which C3 strategies work best for you
- How your relationship with spending is shifting
- Proof that you are building new habits

---

## How to Use This Log

When you feel a strong urge to buy something but resist it:

1. Write down what you almost bought
2. Note which pattern was active (if you recognize one)
3. Identify which C3 strategy helped you resist (or what else worked)
4. Capture how you are feeling now that you did not buy it

Do not wait until the end of the day. Log it in the moment or as soon as possible after. The immediacy matters.

## Column Definitions

**Date**: When the urge happened

**Item**: What you almost bought (be specific)

**Price**: How much it cost (if you know)

**Pattern**: Which Chasing Pattern was active (Rainbows, Unicorns, Ambrosia, Stardust, Sunshine, Moonbeams) or your own language. Leave blank if no pattern fits.

**C3 Alternative Used**: Which C3 strategy helped you resist?
- CUT: "Reminded myself I already reduced coffee to 2x/week"
- CANCEL: "Already canceled this type of subscription"
- COMBINE: "Checked what I already own first"
- Other: "Used comfort menu instead" / "Texted accountability buddy" / "Applied 24-hour rule"

**Why I Didn't Buy**: What stopped you in the moment?
- Examples: "Checked my C3 commitments" / "Noticed I was bored, not actually wanting this" / "Realized it's a Rainbows purchase" / "Remembered my goal" / "Checked budget first"

**Feeling Now**: How do you feel now that you did not buy it?
- Examples: "Relieved" / "Proud" / "Still wanting it but okay" / "Grateful I paused" / "Neutral"

## Weekly Review Prompt

At the end of each week, review this log and answer:

**How many purchases did I resist this week?** _____

**Which C3 strategy worked best?**

**Which pattern showed up most frequently?**

**What does this tell me about my spending triggers?**

---

## What This Log Reveals Over Time

After a few weeks, patterns emerge:

**High-frequency patterns**: If you are logging the same pattern over and over (e.g., Rainbows or Ambrosia), that is your primary spending trigger. You can design future C3 strategies specifically around it.

**Time-of-day patterns**: If most entries happen at night, or mid-afternoon, or weekends, you have identified a trigger window. You can plan ahead for those times.

**C3 strategy effectiveness**: If one C3 path (CUT, CANCEL, COMBINE) keeps showing up in the "Alternative Used" column, that strategy is working for you. Keep using it.

**Emotional patterns**: If "Feeling Now" trends toward relief, pride, or calm, you are building positive associations with not spending. If it trends toward frustration or deprivation, your C3 strategies might be too rigid.

## Celebrate the Wins

Every entry in this log is a win.

You noticed. You paused. You chose differently.

That is the work.

Do not dismiss it because it feels small. Small repeated actions create lasting change.

At the end of 30 days, count how many purchases you resisted. Add up the dollar amounts. That is money that stayed in your account because you built the habit of pausing.

That matters.
