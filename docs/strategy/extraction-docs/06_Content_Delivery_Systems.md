### Daily Post Structure (Skool)
**Source**: Files 3, 4, 18

**Post Title Format**:
"Day X: [Theme] (3-5 words)"

Example: "Day 12: Meeting Your Resistance"

**Post Body**:
1. **Context Paragraph** (3-4 lines)
   - What this day is about
   - Why it matters
   - What to notice

2. **Main Prompt**
   - The reflection question
   - Open-ended, non-judgmental
   - Tied to Lab Loop phase and Pillar

3. **Optional Secondary Prompt**
   - Action step if relevant
   - Alternative angle
   - Pattern-specific question

4. **Data Check-In**
   - □ No-spend day | □ Spent (what: ___ $: ___)
   - Pattern noticed: ___
   - Energy level: 1 2 3 4 5
   - One word for today: ___

5. **Engagement Questions**
   - "What came up for you today?"
   - "Anyone else experiencing this?"
   - "Share your wins or struggles below"

**Posting Schedule**:
- Posted at 6am (user's timezone - Skool handles this)
- Available all day for asynchronous engagement
- No time pressure

## Email Delivery System

### Email + Skool Hybrid Model
**Source**: Files 3, 4

**Evolution of Thinking**:
1. ❌ **Initial idea**: Post daily in Facebook group
2. ❌ **Revision 1**: Daily emails with full content
3. ✅ **Final model**: Skool posts + email notifications

**Why This Model**:
- Content lives in one place (Skool) - single source of truth
- Emails don't get lost or deleted
- Community engagement happens where content is
- Email drives traffic to community (increases stickiness)
- Reduces content duplication/version control issues

**Quote from File 3**:
> "I think the daily content should live in the classroom. And then there's like an automated email that goes out every day that says, 'Hey, today's lesson is live in the classroom.'"

---

### Daily Notification Email Structure
**Source**: Files 3, 4

**Subject Line**:
"Day X: [Theme] - Your no-spend prompt is ready"

**Email Body** (short - 100-150 words):

```
Good morning [Name],

Today is Day 12 of your no-spend challenge.

Today's theme: Meeting Your Resistance

The novelty has worn off. You're in it now. Emotions, boredom, and the voice that says "I've earned this" may get louder. That's not failure. That's the work.

Today's prompt explores what triggers your urge to spend and how to build alternatives that meet the need without money.

👉 See today's full prompt in the classroom: [link]

Check in with the community - you're not doing this alone.

— [Your name]

P.S. Spent yesterday? That's data, not failure. Record it and keep going.
```

**Email Frequency**:
- Daily during active challenges (30-day cycles)
- Weekly during off-challenge periods
- Plus nurture sequences for non-members

### Email Automation Setup
**Source**: Files 3, 4

**Tools Needed**:
- Email service provider (ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, etc.)
- Skool integration or Zapier
- Scheduling system

**Daily Challenge Email Sequence** (30 emails):
- Pre-written and scheduled
- Day 1-30 notifications
- Automatically sent at 6am user's timezone
- Links to corresponding Skool post
- Can pause/unpause sequence if user misses days

**Segmentation**:
- FREE tier: Gets notifications for quarterly challenges only
- PAID tier: Gets notifications for all challenges
- VIP tier: Gets notifications + exclusive content
- Non-members: Gets nurture emails inviting them to join

## Challenge Delivery Models

### 30-Day No-Spend Challenge (Main Product)
**Source**: Files 2, 3, 4, 18

**Delivery Format**: Hybrid (Skool + Standalone)

**For Skool Members**:
1. Join challenge cohort (start date)
2. Receive daily emails (notifications)
3. Access daily Skool posts
4. Engage with community
5. Track progress in Skool or guide
6. Weekly check-ins in community

**For Standalone Guide Buyers**:
1. Purchase and download guide (PDF)
2. Self-directed through 30 days
3. Optional: Join FREE Skool for community support
4. Use guide for prompts and tracking
5. No daily emails (unless they opt in)

**Hybrid Users** (best experience):
- Have the guide (reference, tracking)
- In Skool community (support, engagement)
- Get daily emails (reminders, accountability)
- Use what works for them

### Business Edition Delivery
**Source**: File 16

**Tri-Hybrid Model**:

**1. Workbook (Google Doc)**
- Shareable link sent via email
- User makes copy to their Google Drive
- Self-directed completion
- Can print if desired

**2. Journal (PDF)**
- Download link sent via email
- Daily prompts and reflections
- Can use digitally or print

**3. Spreadsheet Pack (Google Sheets - Optional Add-On)**
- Separate purchase or bundle
- Shareable link via email
- User makes copy
- Pre-filled with formulas and sample data

**Quick Start Roadmap (PDF)**
- 1-3 page orientation guide
- "What do I do first, second, third?"
- Ties all three components together
- Prevents overwhelm

**Support**:
- Email support for 30 days
- Optional: Skool community access (if they join)
- No live calls (unless VIP tier)

## Multi-Format Participation

### Format Options by User Type
**Source**: Files 15, 18

**Visual Learners**:
- Print the guide and mark it up
- Use stickers on habit trackers
- Create Pinterest boards for alternatives
- Watch the challenge unfold visually in Skool

**Audio Learners**:
- Record voice memos for prompts
- Listen to others share in community
- Talk through challenges with accountability partner
- Audio process while driving/walking

**Kinesthetic Learners**:
- Check boxes and track data
- Physically print and use worksheets
- Move through the challenge with actions
- Use "do instead of buy" micro-challenges

**Social Learners**:
- Engage heavily in Skool community
- Find accountability partners
- Share daily in social media
- Join live co-working (VIP)

**Solo Learners**:
- Use standalone guide independently
- Minimal Skool engagement
- Private reflection
- Email-only notifications
