## Competitive Landscape Research

### Prompt 1: Current No-Spend Challenge Landscape
```
What are the most popular no-spend challenges, programs, or courses available in 2025-2026?

Include:
- Who's running them (creators, platforms, brands)
- Format (challenge, course, membership, book)
- Price points
- Unique angles or differentiators
- Community size (if available)
- What makes each one stand out

Focus on: Personal finance space, budgeting influencers, financial wellness brands.
```

**Why This Question**: Know who we're competing with and what they're already doing.

---

### Prompt 2: What's Missing in Current Offerings
```
Based on reviews, complaints, and discussions about no-spend challenges, what are the most common criticisms or gaps people identify?

Look for:
- "I tried [program] but it didn't work because..."
- Common reasons people quit or fail
- What people wish existed but doesn't
- Frustrations with existing programs
- Accessibility issues (cost, time, complexity)

Sources: Reddit (r/Frugal, r/personalfinance, r/nobuy), YouTube comments, blog reviews, social media discussions.
```

**Why This Question**: Identify what competitors are doing wrong so we can do it right.

---

### Prompt 3: Success Stories & What Made Them Work
```
Find case studies, success stories, or research on what makes no-spend challenges successful for participants.

Include:
- What support structures helped people succeed
- What accountability methods work best
- What frameworks or approaches led to lasting behavior change
- Common traits of people who complete challenges vs those who don't
- External factors (community, tracking, education, etc.)

Focus on: Evidence-based findings, not just anecdotes.
```

**Why This Question**: Learn what actually works so we can build those elements in.

## Differentiation & Positioning

### Prompt 4: Pattern Recognition vs Willpower Approaches
```
Compare no-spend or budgeting programs that focus on:
1. Willpower, restriction, and discipline
2. Psychology, pattern recognition, and behavior change

Which approach has better long-term outcomes? What does research say about sustainable behavior change in spending habits?

Include: Academic research, psychology studies, financial behavior research.
```

**Why This Question**: Validate our "pattern recognition over willpower" positioning with evidence.

---

### Prompt 5: Shame-Free vs Traditional Budgeting Messaging
```
How do financial wellness programs currently address shame, guilt, and failure around spending?

Compare:
- Traditional budgeting language (restriction, discipline, "good" vs "bad" spending)
- Modern approaches (shame-free, data-focused, neutral language)

Which messaging resonates better with audiences? What does behavioral psychology say about shame-based vs curiosity-based approaches to financial change?
```

**Why This Question**: Confirm that our anti-shame, "you can't do this wrong" positioning is differentiated and effective.

### Prompt 6: Business vs Personal No-Spend Programs

Are there any no-spend challenges, courses, or programs specifically designed for business owners, solopreneurs, or entrepreneurs?

- Price points and business models

If no:

- What would a business-focused no-spend challenge need to include?

## How to Use These Prompts

### Research Workflow:

1. **Prioritize**: Start with prompts most critical to immediate decisions
   - Competitive landscape (Prompts 1-3)
   - Differentiation (Prompts 4-6)
   - Pricing validation (Prompt 20)

2. **Batch by category**: Run all prompts in one category at once
   - Save results in organized folders
   - Tag findings by relevance (high/medium/low)

3. **Synthesize findings**: After research, create summary documents
   - Key differentiators we can own
   - Market gaps to fill
   - Messaging angles backed by data
   - Tactical changes to make

4. **Update strategy documents**: Use research to inform
   - Marketing launch strategy
   - GPT instructions refinements
   - Landing page copy updates
   - Email sequence improvements

### Perplexity Tips:

- **Use "Focus" mode** for academic research (Prompts 8, 11, 12, 13, 28, 29, 30)
- **Use "Writing" mode** for synthesis and summary
- **Ask follow-up questions** to dig deeper on interesting findings
- **Request sources** to verify claims
- **Save searches** for later reference
