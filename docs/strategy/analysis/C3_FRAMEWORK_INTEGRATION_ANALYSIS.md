---

## C3 Framework Definitions (Reference)

- **CUT**: Reduce frequency or eliminate partially
  *Example: daily coffee → twice a week*

- **CANCEL**: Stop paying entirely OR downgrade to lower tier
  *Example: premium subscription → basic tier, or cancel completely*

- **COMBINE**: Consolidate services, share costs, or replace expensive option with cheaper alternative
  *Example: family streaming plan, bulk buying club, carpool*

---

## Executive Summary

The C3 Framework is **currently absent** from all No-Spend Challenge materials. The existing content uses scattered language about "reducing," "pausing," "canceling," and "consolidating" without organizing these actions under a unified, memorable framework.

**Key Finding**: C3 Framework would function as the **practical action layer** between:
- **Recognition Layer** (Chasing Patterns - identifying WHY you spend)
- **Execution Layer** (tracking, spreadsheets, daily check-ins)

## C3 Framework Benefits Analysis

### Why C3 Works for This Product

1. **Simplicity**: 3 clear categories vs. vague "reduce spending"
2. **Memorability**: Alliterative (Cut, Cancel, Combine) - easy to recall in trigger moments
3. **Non-Binary**: Offers spectrum of options, not all-or-nothing
4. **Actionable**: Each category has clear behavioral outcomes
5. **Flexible**: Users can choose which C3 strategy fits their life
6. **Shame-Free**: Aligns with core philosophy - it's about choosing strategy, not failure/success

### How C3 Complements Existing Frameworks

**Chasing Patterns (Recognition)** → **C3 Framework (Action)** → **Tracking (Data)**

- **Chasing Patterns** answer: "WHY am I spending?" (emotional/psychological)
- **C3 Framework** answers: "WHAT do I do about it?" (tactical decisions)
- **Tracking** answers: "DID it work?" (measurement)

C3 doesn't replace Chasing Patterns - it works WITH them.

### C3 and "This Isn't Deprivation" Messaging

Current challenge: Users fear "no-spend" means total restriction.
**C3 Solution**:
- CUT = reduce, not eliminate (still allows some spending)
- CANCEL = strategic stopping (premium to basic is still CANCEL, but not total deprivation)
- COMBINE = finding creative solutions (not just "do without")

## C3 Language Style Guide

### How to Write About C3

**DO**:
- Use active voice: "CUT your coffee runs to 2x per week"
- Keep definitions simple and example-heavy
- Connect to Chasing Patterns when relevant: "When Chasing Rainbows shows up, try CANCEL strategies"
- Frame as flexible choices: "Which C3 strategy fits this pattern?"
- Use in data check-ins: "C3 strategy for next time: □ CUT  □ CANCEL  □ COMBINE"

**DON'T**:
- Over-explain or make it academic
- Force C3 into every single sentence
- Use it as replacement for Chasing Patterns (they're complementary)
- Present it as rigid rules (it's a decision framework, not commandments)
- Add it where it doesn't naturally fit

### Sample C3 Language

**Introduction**:
> "The C3 Framework gives you three simple paths for any spending decision: CUT (reduce how often), CANCEL (stop entirely or downgrade), or COMBINE (share costs or find cheaper alternatives)."

**In Pattern Descriptions**:
> "When you notice Chasing Rainbows (buying resources for future potential), try CANCEL strategies: pause new course purchases, unsubscribe from bundle emails, stop collecting."

**In Daily Prompts**:
> "If you spent today, what C3 strategy would help prevent this pattern? Could you CUT the frequency, CANCEL the subscription, or COMBINE with someone else?"

**In Worksheets**:
> "Which subscriptions can you CANCEL entirely? Which can you CUT to a lower tier? Which could you COMBINE with family or friends?"
