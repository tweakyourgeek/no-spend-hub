# 7-Day Spending Tracker
## The "Oh No, Where Did It Go?" Edition

*Track your money before it files a missing persons report.*

---

## Hey, You

Let's skip the part where I pretend you're going to revolutionize your finances this week. You're not. Neither am I. We're all just out here buying oat milk and wondering why our bank account looks like that.

But here's the thing: you probably have no idea where your money actually goes. Not really. You've got a vague sense—rent, groceries, that subscription you swore you'd cancel. But the full picture? The $6 here and $14 there that somehow adds up to "wait, I made HOW much this month?"

Yeah. That's what we're looking at this week.

No budgeting. No guilt trips. No Dave Ramsey energy.

Just: where the hell is all this money going?

## The Only Rule

**Write down every single thing you buy.**

- The coffee (yes, that one)
- The Amazon order you already forgot about
- The thing at Target you didn't need but it was RIGHT THERE
- The subscription you've been paying for since 2019
- The snack you grabbed because gas stations are designed by evil geniuses

Everything. For 7 days.

That's it. That's the whole challenge.

---

## What You're NOT Doing

- Trying to spend less (lol, not yet)
- Beating yourself up (counterproductive and boring)
- Making a budget (we'll get there, maybe, if we feel like it)
- Being "good with money" (let's just start with "aware of money")

You're conducting a scientific experiment. The subject is your bank account. The hypothesis is that you're probably spending money on things you forgot about five seconds after buying them.

Let's test that.

## Day-by-Day (Because Apparently We Need Structure)

### Day 1: Just Start

**Mission:** Track everything. Don't be a hero about it.

Phone notes, actual paper, the tracker above—whatever you'll actually use. Every time you spend money, write it down.

Include how you felt. "Fine" doesn't count. Dig a little. Tired? Annoyed? Weirdly happy? Avoiding something?

*End of day:* How many purchases? Were any of them things you genuinely forgot about by the time you wrote them down?

---

### Day 2: Autopilot Mode

**Mission:** Catch yourself doing The Thing.

You know The Thing. The automatic purchase. The one you don't even think about anymore. Coffee on the way to work. The vending machine. Adding something to your cart because shipping is free over $35 and you're at $28.

Don't stop yourself. Just notice: "Oh, I'm doing The Thing."

*End of day:* What's your The Thing? How much is it costing you per week?

---

### Day 3: Feelings, Ugh

**Mission:** Pay attention to what happens BEFORE you buy.

Today, every time you're about to spend money, pause for literally 2 seconds and notice what you're feeling. Bored? Hungry? Like you deserve a treat? Stressed about that email?

Then buy the thing anyway if you want. We're not stopping anything. Just noticing.

*End of day:* What emotion showed up the most? Is it the one you expected?

---

### Day 4: The Midweek Reckoning

**Mission:** Look at your numbers. Don't freak out.

Add up what you've spent so far if you want. Or don't. Either way, scroll through your list.

Any surprises? Anything you forgot? Anything that made you go "wait, I spent money on THAT?"

This isn't about judgment. It's about the mild horror of seeing things written down.

*End of day:* What's the most "what was I thinking" purchase so far?

---

### Day 5: Need vs. Want vs. "Unclear"

**Mission:** Start categorizing.

For today's purchases, ask yourself: need, want, or "I genuinely don't know why I bought this."

No shame in wants. Wants are fine. We're humans, not robots. But it's useful to know the ratio.

*End of day:* What's your need/want/mystery breakdown? Any patterns?

---

### Day 6: The Satisfaction Question

**Mission:** Check how long the good feeling lasts.

When you buy something today, notice: How long does the happy last?

- Did you forget about it by the time you got to your car?
- Still enjoying it an hour later?
- Already regretting it?

This is about figuring out which purchases actually do something for you and which ones are just... noise.

*End of day:* Best emotional ROI purchase vs. worst. GO.

---

### Day 7: The Big Picture

**Mission:** Zoom out. See the whole mess. Appreciate it.

Last day. Keep tracking, but also review the whole week.

Look for:
- Where most of your money went
- Your most common pre-purchase emotion
- The need/want ratio
- Anything you're still thinking about (vs. already forgot)
- Your biggest "oh" moment

*End of day:* If you had to describe your spending personality in a dating profile, what would it say?

---

## You Did It. Now What?

Congrats. You just did something most people avoid forever: you looked at where your money actually goes.

You didn't fix anything. You didn't budget. You didn't become a finance bro. You just... saw it.

That's step one. Everything else is easier after that.

### Your Options:

**1. Tell Someone**
Share your biggest revelation with the community. Bonus points if it's embarrassing. (Spoiler: everyone's is.)

**2. Pick ONE Thing to Mess With**
Don't overhaul your life. Just pick one thing:
- One subscription to cancel
- One autopilot habit to question
- One emotional trigger to watch

Baby steps. We're not curing capitalism here.

**3. Go Deeper If You're Feeling Bold**
The 30-Day Challenge is there if you want it. No pressure. It'll be there when you're ready. Or never. We don't judge.
