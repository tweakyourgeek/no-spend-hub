# 7-Day Spending Tracker
## The Awareness Edition

*Before you change anything, see everything.*

---

## Welcome

You're not here to restrict yourself. You're not here to feel guilty. You're here to get curious.

For the next 7 days, you're going to do one simple thing: **notice where your money goes**. That's it. No cutting back. No budgeting. No shame spirals.

Just awareness.

Because here's what most money advice gets wrong—it asks you to change before you understand. That's like trying to fix a car without opening the hood.

This week, you open the hood.

## How This Works

### The Rules (There's Only One)

**Track every purchase. Every single one.**

- The $4 coffee
- The $1.29 app
- The $47 impulse buy
- The bills on autopay
- The "I forgot about that" subscriptions

Write it down. Log it in your phone. Use the tracker below. Whatever works—just capture it.

### What You're NOT Doing

- Judging yourself
- Trying to spend less
- Feeling bad about what you find
- Changing any habits (yet)

You're a scientist this week. Scientists observe. They don't judge the data.

## Day-by-Day Guide

### Day 1: Just Begin

Today's focus: **Start tracking. Don't overthink it.**

Grab your phone, a notebook, or use the tracker above. Every time you spend money, write it down. Include:
- What you bought
- How much
- One word for how you felt (tired, happy, bored, rushed, etc.)

That's it. You're just building the habit of noticing.

*End of day reflection:* How many purchases did you make today? Were any surprising?

---

### Day 2: Notice the Autopilot

Today's focus: **Catch the automatic spending.**

Some purchases happen without thinking—the morning coffee, the convenience store stop, the "add to cart" muscle memory. Today, try to catch yourself in autopilot mode.

You don't have to stop it. Just notice: "Oh, I'm doing that thing again."

*End of day reflection:* What's your most automatic purchase? The one you don't even think about?

---

### Day 3: Follow the Feelings

Today's focus: **Connect spending to emotions.**

Look at your "How I Felt" column from Days 1-2. Any patterns? Did you spend more when tired? Stressed? After a hard conversation?

Today, pay extra attention to what happens RIGHT BEFORE you buy something. What triggered it?

*End of day reflection:* What emotion most often shows up before you spend?

---

### Day 4: The Midweek Check-In

Today's focus: **Review without judgment.**

You're halfway there. Take 5 minutes to look at your tracker so far.

Add up your totals if you want, but don't attach meaning to the number. It's just data. Some weeks are higher. Some are lower. This is ONE week of your life.

What matters: Are you seeing patterns? Things you forgot you spent on? Purchases that don't match how you want to feel?

*End of day reflection:* What's one thing you've noticed that surprised you?

---

### Day 5: Need vs. Want (No Judgment Version)

Today's focus: **Get curious about the "Needed or Wanted" column.**

This isn't about shaming your wants. Wants are human. But noticing the ratio helps.

For today's purchases, pause before each one and ask: "Is this a need or a want?" Then track it honestly. No wrong answers.

*End of day reflection:* What's your need-to-want ratio looking like? (Again, no judgment. Just noticing.)

---

### Day 6: The Time Question

Today's focus: **Notice how long the good feeling lasts.**

When you buy something today, notice: How long does the satisfaction last?

- 5 minutes?
- An hour?
- All day?
- Already forgot about it?

This isn't about guilt. It's about return on investment—emotional ROI.

*End of day reflection:* Which purchase gave you the longest-lasting good feeling?

---

### Day 7: See the Whole Picture

Today's focus: **Step back and observe.**

Last day. Keep tracking, but also take time to review your full week.

Look for:
- Your biggest spending category
- Your most common emotion before purchases
- The need/want breakdown
- Any purchases you completely forgot about
- Anything that surprised you

*End of day reflection:* If you could describe your spending pattern in one sentence, what would it be?

## After 7 Days: What Now?

You did it. You watched. You tracked. You stayed curious instead of critical.

Now you have something most people don't: **actual data about your actual life.**

- One subscription to pause
- One autopilot purchase to question
- One emotional trigger to watch
