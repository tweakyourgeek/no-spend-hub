## EMAIL 2: Day 5 — The Thing Most People Miss

**Subject Line Options:**
1. The expense most business owners forget to track
2. You're probably missing this in your bookkeeping
3. The category nobody sets up (and it matters)

---

There is a category of business expense that almost nobody tracks properly. It is not office supplies or software subscriptions. Those are obvious.

It is the "I'll figure out where this goes later" pile.

You know the one. The charge that could be marketing or could be professional development. The software that serves two departments. The meal that was sort of a business meeting. The course you bought for your business that you also use personally.

These gray area expenses usually end up in one of two places: a miscellaneous category that grows until it's meaningless, or nowhere at all.

The template has a Notes column for exactly this reason. Use it. Write "not sure if this is marketing or education" and move on. You do not need to decide right now. You need to capture it.

At the end of the month, look at your Notes column. Patterns will show up. You will notice that half your "miscellaneous" expenses are actually the same type of spending. That is useful information. It means you need a category for it.

Your bookkeeping doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be honest. The Notes column is where honest happens.

Lexi

## EMAIL 3: Day 7 — How This Fits Into a Bigger Picture

**Subject Line Options:**
1. What your bookkeeping template is actually telling you
2. The pattern your numbers are showing you
3. Your bookkeeping data means something (here's what)

---

You have been entering numbers for about a week. Maybe you have last month done. Maybe two months. Maybe you got ambitious and went back further.

Here is the part most people skip: looking at what the numbers mean.

Not in a panic way. Not in a "my business is failing" way. In a "huh, that's interesting" way.

Open your dashboard and look at the expense breakdown. Find the category where you're spending the most. Now ask yourself one question: did you know that category was your biggest expense before you saw the number?

Most people are surprised. Not by the total. By the distribution. They assumed marketing was their biggest cost, but it's actually software subscriptions. They thought they were spending a lot on contractors, but it's the small recurring charges that add up.

This is what bookkeeping is actually for. Not tax prep (though it helps with that). Not looking professional (though it does that too). It is for seeing reality clearly enough to make decisions from information instead of anxiety.

The template shows you where money goes. What you do with that information is yours to decide. There is no wrong answer. There is only clarity where there used to be guessing.

Lexi
