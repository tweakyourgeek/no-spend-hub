# Crisis Section - What to Do When You're Stuck

## If You Get Overwhelmed

**Big, unavoidable, different color section**

### Scenario 1: "I have no idea where to start"
→ Open the Roadmap, go to "Before You Start," and do just Section 1 of the Workbook (Financial Inventory). Write rough numbers. That's enough for today.

### Scenario 2: "I'm halfway through and I hate this"
→ Open the Journal. Write one honest page about why you hate it. That still counts as doing the challenge. Then decide: do you want to keep going, or do you want to stop and come back later?

### Scenario 3: "I spent money and now I feel like I failed"
→ You didn't fail. Spending during a no-spend challenge is data, not failure. Go to your current week in the Journal and answer just one prompt: "What was I feeling right before I spent?" That's the work.

### Scenario 4: "I don't know which tool to use when"
→ Here's a quick guide:

| Situation | Use This Tool |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Need structure and decisions** | Workbook |
| **Need to process feelings** | Journal |
| **Need to see numbers visually** | Spreadsheet Pack |
| **Need to talk it out** | Skool Community |

---

## Crisis Exit Option

**You can always:**
- Skip to just the data check-ins (bottom 1/3 of each Journal page)
- Use only the Workbook and ignore the Journal
- Use only the Journal and ignore the Workbook
- Stop, put it down, and come back in a week
- Decide this challenge isn't for you right now

**All of those are valid.** There's no wrong way to engage with this work.

---

*Design note: This section should be visually distinct from the rest of the roadmap - different background color, larger text, or a highlighted box. It needs to feel like an "emergency exit" that's always available, not just another step.*
