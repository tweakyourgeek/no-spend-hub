# Four-Step Detailed Content Blocks

## Step 1: Before You Start - Prep & Setup

**What you're doing:** Setting up the experiment. You're not committing to perfection, you're choosing what to test.

### In the Workbook:
- **Section 1 (Financial Inventory):** Do a light pass. Rough numbers are fine. You're getting a snapshot, not building a detailed budget.
- **Section 2 (Your Direction):** Name what you want to learn and what you're willing to test for 30 days.
- **Section 3 (Rules & Boundaries):** Pick 2-4 rules that feel like experiments, not punishments. Remember: you can adjust these mid-challenge if needed.

### In the Journal:
- **"Before You Begin" section:** Complete the initial prompts to establish your starting point.
- **Money Feelings Mind Map:** Map out how you feel about money right now. This is your emotional baseline.
- **First Word Cloud + Word of the Week:** Choose your anchor word for Week 1.

**Important:** You don't need to complete every page to get value from this challenge. If a prompt doesn't land, skip it. If spreadsheets make you want to throw your laptop, ignore them. The challenge works because you're paying attention, not because you filled in every blank.

**Quick reminder:** Spending during a no-spend challenge doesn't mean you failed. It means you have data to work with.

---

## Step 2: Week 1 - Noticing & Naming

**What you're doing:** Observing without judgment. You're learning what your spending patterns look like in real time.

### In the Journal:
- **Week 1 daily pages:** Use these to track urges, surprises, and first patterns. Notice what comes up when you interrupt familiar spending habits.
- **Week 1 Wrap-Up:** End-of-week reflection to identify what you learned in the first seven days.

### In the Workbook:
- **First Weekly Check-in:** Log your week. Keep numbers approximate if you need to. Precision matters less than participation.

**If you spend money this week:** That's data, not failure. Write down what happened, what you were feeling, what triggered it. That's the work.

---

## Step 3: Weeks 2-3 - Urges & Patterns

**What you're doing:** Going deeper. This is where patterns start to reveal themselves, and where the challenge might feel harder.

### In the Journal:
- **Week 2 (Urges & Alternatives):** Work through daily pages focused on identifying spending triggers and building non-spending alternatives.
- **Week 3 (Patterns & Pressure Points):** Daily pages explore your specific patterns and where external/internal pressure shows up.
- **Weekly Wrap-Ups:** Complete end-of-week reflections for both weeks.

### In the Workbook:
- **Weekly Check-ins:** Continue tracking each week's spending and patterns.
- **Optional: Spreadsheet Pack:** If you're a numbers person, use the weekly overview and before/after comparison tabs to see visual progress.

**This is the heavy section.** If it feels overwhelming:
- Focus on the Journal OR the Workbook, not both.
- Use voice memos instead of writing if that's easier.
- Skip days that don't serve you. You can still get value from partial participation.

---

## Step 4: Week 4 & Beyond - Integration & Next Steps

**What you're doing:** Deciding what to keep. Not all of it, not none of it. Just what actually serves you.

### In the Journal:
- **Week 4 (Integration & Self-Respect):** Daily pages focused on consolidating what you've learned and practicing self-respect around money.
- **End-of-Challenge Reflection:** Big-picture review of the full 30 days.

### In the Workbook:
- **Section 5 (Debrief):** Review your before/after, name what shifted, identify what you want to carry forward.
- **Section 6 (Spreadsheet Tie-ins) - if applicable:** If you used the Spreadsheet Pack, review your dashboards and freed-up money allocation.

**After the challenge:** You don't need to have all the answers yet. Pick 1-2 gentle experiments to try in the next 30 days. That's enough.

---

*Design note: Each of these steps should be expandable/collapsible sections or separate page sections that users can navigate to via the wayfinding navigator. Consider adding visual dividers or cards to separate the Journal tasks from Workbook tasks within each step.*
