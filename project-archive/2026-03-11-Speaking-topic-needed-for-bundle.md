## Assistant

**5-Part Structure:**

**PROS:**
- **Explicit recognition phase** is psychologically crucial - people need to consciously identify "I do this" before they can shift to "here's why/what else this could be"
- Follows natural change progression: invitation → awareness → alternative → application → integration
- Gives space for the "oh shit that's me" moment separate from the solution, which makes the reframe land harder
- Each phase has ONE job = lower cognitive load per section
- Mirrors therapeutic practice (acknowledge current state → introduce new frame → practice → integrate)

**CONS:**
- 5 transitions in 10 minutes = potential for feeling rushed/choppy
- Risk of not spending enough time in any phase for it to stick
- More cognitive load from switching between phases frequently
- Could feel too structured/didactic vs. organic

---

**4-Part Structure:**

**PROS:**
- Smoother flow = easier to follow in compressed timeframe
- Combining recognition + reframe mirrors how insight often happens naturally (see problem + solution simultaneously)
- More time per phase = deeper processing/integration
- Easier to manage pacing in 10 minutes
- Feels more conversational, less step-by-step

**CONS:**
- **Psychologically risky**: if recognition gets rushed inside the reframe section, people might not land in "this is me" long enough to feel the shift
- The reframe section has to do two jobs (name current state + offer new lens) which could overload it
- People might intellectually understand without emotionally connecting to their own pattern
- Skips the important pause between "I see myself" and "here's a different way"

---

**My take from psychology:**

The 5-part structure is more sound psychologically because **recognition needs its own space**. People have to consciously acknowledge "I do this thing" before the reframe can land. Without that explicit recognition phase, you risk them staying in observer mode ("interesting perspective") vs. participant mode ("holy shit this is about ME").

But - timing is tight. So maybe the compromise is: **keep 5 phases but weight them strategically**. Give Recognition the time it needs (even if brief), then the Reframe can be shorter because they've already landed in awareness.

# New Lens Workshop Framework

## Purpose
New Lens Workshops are micro-presentations (10 minutes) that invite participants to recognize patterns and reframe their perspective through a new lens. Unlike Quick Win Workshops (which focus on completing a task), these focus on introducing awareness and providing tools for continued exploration.

## Core Structure (10 minutes)

### 1. Invitation (1-2 min)
Here's something familiar you might be experiencing
- Creates the opening for recognition
- Non-threatening entry point
- "What if there's another way to look at this?"

### 2. Recognition (1-2 min)
If this sounds like you, here's what might be happening / Here's the pattern you might be in
- Explicit acknowledgment phase
- "Do you see yourself here?"
- Creates the "oh shit that's me" moment
- Psychologically crucial - people need to consciously identify "I do this" before they can shift

### 3. The Lens (2-3 min)
Here's a different way to look at this / This is what the lens reveals
- Introduces the reframe
- Shows what becomes visible through this lens
- Not prescriptive, exploratory

### 4. Lived Reality (3-4 min)
What this looks like in practice / How this shows up in real situations
- Concrete examples
- Recognition prompts
- "Here's what this pattern looks like in the wild"

### 5. Integration (1-2 min)
What this means for you + handout as your continued exploration tool + next step
- Summary
- Handout introduction
- Soft CTA / next step

## Key Principles

- **Tone**: Invitation, not instruction
- **Goal**: Pattern recognition, not problem-solving
- **Outcome**: Awareness + tool to continue exploring (the handout)
- **Philosophy**: "You might want to try this lens on and see if it fits"
- **The work happens AFTER**: The workshop plants the seed; integration happens later

## Two-Handout System

### Handout 1: Workshop Notes (During)
**Purpose**: Active listening scaffold for processing IN THE MOMENT

**Structure** - Following the 5 phases with active listening prompts:

**Invitation:**
- What did I hear? (paraphrasing)
- What feelings came up? (verbalizing emotions)

**Recognition:**
- What patterns do I recognize? (noting cues)
- Questions I have: (clarifying)

**The Lens:**

**Lived Reality:**
- Examples I'm noticing: (noting cues)
- What else do I want to understand? (encouraging/clarifying)

**Integration:**
- What's landing for me? (listen for total meaning)
- Next steps/questions: (waiting to disclose - holding space for their own processing)

**Dual Purpose:**
- Workshop engagement tool

- Encouraging
- Non-verbal affirmations

|---|---|
| 90 minutes | 10 minutes |
| "Do this thing, get this result RIGHT NOW" | "Here's a lens to try on" |
| Completion-focused | Awareness-focused |
| Transformation arc | Introduction arc |
