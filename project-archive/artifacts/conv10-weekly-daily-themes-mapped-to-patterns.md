## New 5-Section Structure (Replaces PLR Architecture)

### Section 1: Your Money Story (Pages 1-3)
- Welcome, context, and why a 30-day pause
- Introduce the Chasing Series as the lens (not the lesson)
- Brief money-memory and "how money feels now" prompts

### Section 2: Spot Your Chasing Pattern (Pages 4-6)
- One clean diagnostic spread:
  - Ambrosia, Rainbows, Unicorns, Stardust, Sunshine, Moonbeams (short blurbs, 2-3 "this might be you if..." each)
- "Which patterns feel most like you right now?" checklist
- Bridge text: "Your rules and daily rituals will be based on the patterns you circled."

### Section 3: Design Your 30-Day Container (Pages 7-9)
- Replace SMART and PLR-style preparation with:
  - "Your Numbers Snapshot" (very lightweight)
  - "Pattern-based rules & safeguards" (different suggestions per dominant pattern)
  - "Support & environment" (how you'll make it easier to keep those rules)

### Section 4: The 30-Day Ritual (Weekly overviews + daily pages)
- Weekly themes aligned with the Challenge Arc:
  - Entry / Reset (Rainbows, Unicorns)
  - Early Resistance (Ambrosia)
  - Mid-Challenge (Stardust, Sunshine)
  - Late Challenge (Moonbeams)
- Each day = context + prompt; days are written fresh, informed by which pattern tends to activate at that phase

### Section 5: Integration & Next Cycle (Pages 42-45)
- Reflect on wins, slips, and pattern sightings
- "Choose Your Next Cycle" paths based on which patterns showed up most
- Gentle "what's next" plus related tools/products

## Rewrite Approach (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1:** Lock the table of contents. Draft a one-page outline with section titles, subsections, and which Chasing patterns are primary in each part.

**Step 2:** Write teaching sections first (Sections 1-3 & 5). Ignore old text. Use the Chasing Series canonical map and the challenge arc. Aim for lean, workbook-length teaching; the "work" happens in prompts.

**Step 3:** Build the 30 daily pages to match the arc. For each week, decide which pattern(s) are foreground, then write 7 prompts that help notice, interrupt, or re-route that pattern in real scenarios.
