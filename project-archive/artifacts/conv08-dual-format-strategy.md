## Why you need both

**The book version** is for people who:
- Print their workbooks and don't want to keep toggling to a browser
- Want to scribble notes in margins
- Need a "what order do I do this in?" quick reference while they're using the physical/PDF tool

**The site version** is for people who:
- Are confused and need wayfinding ("where am I? what do I do next?")
- Want something you can update without re-issuing files
- Need the "stuck/overwhelmed" intervention moment
- Prefer clicking around to flipping pages

They serve **different moments in the user experience**, so redundancy isn't a bug—it's a feature.

---

## What goes in each

### In the Workbook & Journal (static reference)

Keep it to **one page max**, very stripped-down:

**Heading:** How to Use This Challenge

**4 steps** (use your existing micro-copy):
1. Before You Start – do X, Y
2. Week 1 – do X, Y
3. Weeks 2-3 – do X, Y
4. Week 4 & Beyond – do X, Y

**One-line footer:**
> Lost? Overwhelmed? Visit the interactive roadmap: [link to Canva site]

That's it. You're not trying to fit the whole experience in the book. You're just giving people enough structure to not feel adrift when they open the PDF.

**Where it lives in each book:**
- **Workbook:** Page 2 or 3, right after the title page, before Section 1
- **Journal:** Inside front cover or first right-hand page

---

### On the Canva site (interactive hub)

**Sections:**

1. **Hero**
   - "Your 30-Day Business No Spend Roadmap"
   - One sentence: "This is your map through the Workbook, Journal, and Spreadsheet Pack. You can't do it wrong."

2. **Three pillar boxes**
   - Workbook: "Structure, tracking, before/after"
   - Journal: "Daily noticing, feelings, patterns"
   - Spreadsheet Pack: "Optional. For people who like numbers."
   - Each links to download/access

3. **"Where am I?" wayfinding**
   - Checkbox-style or button navigation:
     - Haven't started -> Before You Start
     - Days 1-7 -> Week 1
     - Days 8-21 -> Weeks 2-3
     - Days 22-30+ -> Week 4 & Beyond
     - Stuck/overwhelmed -> Crisis section

4. **Four step tiles**
   - Your existing Step 1-4 blocks, expanded with all the context
   - These are collapsible or separate pages

5. **"If you get overwhelmed" strip**
   - Big, unavoidable, different color
   - "Open the Journal. Write one honest page. That still counts."

6. **Footer**
   - Link to Skool community or wherever they ask questions
   - "Last updated: [date]" so people know it's current

## How to prevent version drift

1. **The site is the source of truth.** When you update the challenge, update the site first.

2. **The book version is intentionally minimal.** It's just "here's the order of operations." It doesn't have context, so there's less to keep in sync.

3. **Add a version note in the books:**
   > This roadmap was last updated [Month Year]. For the most current version, visit [Canva site link].

4. **Build a simple update checklist:**
   ```
   When I change the challenge structure:
   - Update Canva site
   - Update Workbook roadmap page (if structure changed)
   - Update Journal roadmap page (if structure changed)
   ```
   If you only changed *context* (like rewording a section), you don't need to touch the books. Only update them if the **order or structure** changes.

---

## How to position them to users

**In your welcome email / first touchpoint:**

> You have three tools for this challenge: the Workbook, the Journal, and (optionally) the Spreadsheet Pack.
>
> **Not sure where to start?** Use this interactive roadmap: [link to Canva site]
>
> The roadmap will help you figure out which tool to use when, and what to do if you get stuck. There's also a quick-reference version inside the Workbook and Journal if you prefer to stay in the book.

**In the books themselves (first page of roadmap):**

> **Prefer an interactive version?**
> This roadmap also lives on the web, where you can jump between sections and get unstuck faster: [link]

**On the Canva site:**

> **Prefer to work from the book?**
> You can find a simplified version of this roadmap on page [X] of the Workbook and Journal.

## Refinement

**Book roadmap = logistics**
"Here's what to do in what order."

**Site roadmap = logistics + psychology**
"Here's what to do in what order AND here's what to do when it feels terrible."

That way they're complementary rather than redundant. The book gets you oriented. The site gets you unstuck.

---

## Bottom line

Yes, put it in both. Make the book version **short and structural**. Make the site version **long and supportive**. Link between them. Update the site freely; only touch the books if the structure changes.

This gives people options without creating confusion, and it lets you iterate the experience without constantly re-issuing PDFs.
