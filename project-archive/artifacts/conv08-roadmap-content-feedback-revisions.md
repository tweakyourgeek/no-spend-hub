# Roadmap Content Feedback and Revisions

## Initial User Submission:

A 4-step roadmap structure:

**Step 1 - Before (Prep):** Workbook light pass on Sections 1-3, Journal Before You Begin + Mind Map + Word Cloud
**Step 2 - Week 1 (Noticing & Naming):** Journal Week 1 daily pages + wrap-up, Workbook first Weekly Check-in
**Step 3 - Weeks 2-3 (Urges & Patterns):** Journal Weeks 2-3 daily pages + wrap-ups, Workbook Weekly Check-ins + optional Spreadsheet Pack
**Step 4 - Week 4 & After (Integration):** Journal Week 4 + End-of-Challenge Reflection, Workbook Section 5 Debrief + Section 6

## Feedback Provided:

### What's Working:
- The 4-step progression is psychologically smart: "set up the experiment" -> "notice what happens" -> "understand the patterns" -> "decide what to keep"
- "Light pass" in the Before section
- "Keep numbers approximate if needed"
- The "If you get stuck" footer
- Specifying which tool to use when
- The optional spreadsheet callout

### Where to Push It:

**1. Make the anti-perfectionism even louder.**
Right now it's whispered in phrases like "optionally" and "if needed." Make it structural:

> **Important:** You don't need to complete every page to get value from this challenge. If a prompt doesn't land, skip it. If spreadsheets make you want to throw your laptop, ignore them. The challenge works because you're paying attention, not because you filled in every blank.

**2. Address the "what if I spend?" elephant immediately.**
This challenge isn't actually about not spending (even though that's the name). It's about noticing. Add to the Before section:

> Quick reminder: Spending during a no-spend challenge doesn't mean you failed. It means you get data about what happened and why. The point is awareness, not perfection.

**3. The Week 2-3 combo might be heavy.**
Two weeks of daily pages plus wrap-ups plus weekly check-ins. That's when people quit. Either:
- Split them into separate steps, OR
- Add explicit "pick one" language: "Use Week 2 OR Week 3 daily pages, or sample from both"

**4. Strengthen the "stuck" footer:**

> **If you get stuck:**
> - Do literally one page from wherever you are. That still counts.
> - Or skip to the Week 4 reflection and work backward from "what do I want to keep?"
> - Or just track your urges on scratch paper for a week. The fancy pages aren't the point.

**5. Consider adding a "choose your own adventure" note:**

> **How to use this roadmap:**
> Linear people: Follow steps 1->2->3->4.
> Chaotic people: Start anywhere that feels interesting. The structure is a suggestion, not a straitjacket.

**6. Shift some "complete/finish" language to "explore/try."**
- "Complete 'Before You Begin'" -> "Try the 'Before You Begin' exercise"
- "Finish Section 5 Debrief" -> "Use Section 5 to reflect"

## Bottom Line:

This is a really functional roadmap that will prevent people from feeling lost in two separate workbooks. The structure is clear without being rigid. Push the "you can't do this wrong" messaging from subtext to main text, because that's the thing that makes this work different from every other challenge out there.
