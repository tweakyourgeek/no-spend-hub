# Business Workbook Review Notes

## Overall Assessment: 90% Done

"This is fucking excellent. The architecture is solid, the tone is perfect, and the prompts are going to hit business owners right where they live."

## What's Working Beautifully:

- **"Every Damn Thing I Paid For"** as a section title - it's the exact internal monologue business owners have when they look at their subscriptions
- **"On the Horizon (Not for This 30-Day Cycle)"** - brilliant permission-giving that prevents overwhelm
- **The duplication awareness questions are surgical:** "Where did I buy something I already had in another form?" and "What was I hoping the new thing would solve that the old one didn't?" Those are going to surface Chasing Ambrosia patterns all over the place
- **Weekly check-ins asking "What did I use that I already had instead?"** reinforces positive replacement behavior, not just tracking failures
- **End debrief focuses on "What I'm Keeping"** not "What I Did Wrong" - stays shame-free all the way through

## What Needs Finishing (Minimal):

1. **Section 0 content** - the headers exist but the actual welcome/how-to-use text isn't written yet. That's probably 30 minutes of writing.
2. **Section 6 detailed callouts** - you have the structure but not the full explanatory text for how the spreadsheet pack integrates. Another 20 minutes.
3. **Optional: Business-specific examples** in a few prompts. Like in "What did I want to buy this week?" you could add an example: "new project management tool, course on email marketing, Canva Pro upgrade."

## The Commitment Statement Format (Section 2.3):

```
During this challenge, my focus is ______ so that ______.
```

Discussion: Does this fill-in-the-blank feel like your voice, or too formulaic? Decision: **Keep it.** It's intentional constraint. Business owners especially will want to say "my focus is reducing subscriptions AND stopping course purchases AND auditing my PLR AND..." The format literally won't let them do that. The "so that" part forces them to articulate the outcome they actually want, not just the behavior change.

## Three Edit Notes:

1. The commitment statement format stays (intentional constraint, user confirmed)
2. Optional: Add business-specific examples in italics to a few prompts
3. What's "missing" is "not written yet" not "needs editing" - the actual prompts, structure, flow, and language are ready to ship
