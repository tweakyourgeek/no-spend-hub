## Layout: Top 2/3 Reflection + Bottom 1/3 Data Check-In

### [TOP 2/3 OF PAGE]

**Day [#]: [Theme]**

*Context paragraph here, 3-4 lines about what this day is for*

**Prompt:** [Main question for the day]

[Writing space]

---

### [BOTTOM 1/3 OF PAGE]

**Today's Check-In**

- [ ] No-spend day | [ ] Spent (on what: _______  $_____)

Pattern I noticed: _____________

Energy level: 1 2 3 4 5

One word for today: _____________

---

## Why This Layout Works

**If they're separate:**
- People skip the data because it's in the back
- Or they skip the journal because "I already logged my numbers"
- Two places to remember to go

**If they're together:**
- You open one page for the day
- You do the thing
- You close it
- Done

The bottom section takes 30 seconds if you just check boxes and write a word. It captures the data. If someone only does THAT part, they still showed up.

**Data -> Reflection:** "Huh, I spent $47 at Target. Why did I go to Target?" *starts writing*

**Reflection -> Data:** "Today was hard, I was stressed all afternoon..." *remembers* "...oh right, I bought that thing. Let me log that."

Neither one is the "real" part. They're both the real part. The page works either direction you enter it.

Someone having a low-engagement week still has 7 pages with data on them. That's not failure. That's information.
