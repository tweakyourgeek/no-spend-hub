# Roadmap Draft Review

## Assessment: Ready

"This Roadmap is **really good**."

## Strong Structural Choices:

### 1. The Day 0 Breakdown with Time Estimates
60-90 minutes total, broken into 5 steps with realistic timing - that makes it feel approachable instead of overwhelming. People can see the whole scope before they commit.

### 2. The "If You Get Overwhelmed" Section
This is the part that will actually keep people from abandoning the whole thing. You're explicitly giving them permission to scale down to "minimum viable participation" instead of pretending overwhelm won't happen.

### 3. Weekly Rhythm is Simple
- Start of week: set word
- During week: daily pages + mid-week glance at workbook
- End of week: check-in + wrap-up + word cloud

That's repeatable. Not complicated.

### 4. It Shows How the Pieces Interact Without Forcing Integration
"If all you do is complete the Workbook and write a few honest pages in the Journal, you are doing this challenge correctly."

That line prevents people from feeling like they're failing if they don't touch the spreadsheets.

## One Optional Enhancement:

Under **"What you have access to"** (right after listing the three core pieces), you *could* add one clarifying line:

> "You do not need to use all three tools to complete this challenge successfully. The Workbook + Journal is a complete system on its own."

But that's polish, not a structural issue.

## This Is Ready.

The Roadmap does exactly what it needs to do: it shows people where to start, how to move through the month, and where to return when they get lost.
