# Scaffolding for Sections 3-5 (from Perplexity)

This scaffolding was created by Perplexity to help draft Sections 3-5 of the No-Spend Guide. It provides questions and structure for each page.

## Section 3 -- Design Your 30-Day Container

### Page 7 -- Your Numbers Snapshot

Goal: Light, non-overwhelming context.

- Short intro
  - "What 2-3 sentences explain that this is a quick look, not a full budget or audit?"
- Snapshot table (broad categories, fill-in)
  - Essentials (housing, utilities, basic groceries).
  - Getting around (transportation).
  - Food out / treats.
  - Shopping / stuff.
  - Subscriptions / digital.
  - Debt + savings.
  - "Other leaks."
- Prompts underneath
  - "Which 1-2 categories feel surprisingly high or 'leaky'?"
  - "Which 1-2 categories feel pretty aligned with how you want your money to behave?"

### Page 8 -- Pattern-Based Rules & Safeguards

Goal: Turn patterns into 2-4 rules.

- Short framing
  - "What one paragraph explains that rules are experiments, not punishments, and should fit their patterns?"
- For each main pattern (you can foreground 3-4 here):
  - **Ambrosia (buying to change feelings)**
    - "Example rules you could offer (e.g., no purchases when X feeling is present, wait period, comfort menu first)."
  - **Rainbows (buying the next fix)**
    - "Example rules (e.g., no buying courses/templates, must re-use something already owned, 30-day pause on 'business investments')."
  - **Unicorns (chasing ideal you)**
    - "Example rules (e.g., no purchases 'to keep up,' one outfit/gear setup only, commit to current system for 30 days)."
  - **Stardust / Sunshine / Moonbeams**
    - "One or two tailored rules each if you want, or a combined block like 'identity & desire rules.'"
- Their rules block
  - "My rules for this 30-day round are:" (4-6 lines with checkboxes).
  - "Which one is non-negotiable? Which one is flexible?"

### Page 9 -- Support, Environment, and Accountability

Goal: Make rules livable.

- People
  - "Who will know you're doing this challenge?"
  - "What kind of support would actually help (check-ins, co-planning, 'don't let me buy X')?"
- Places & tech
  - "What 2-3 simple changes can you make to your environment (apps off phone, cards out of wallet, favorite free activities list visible)?"
- Check-ins
  - "When will you check in with yourself (daily, weekly)? How (journal, wall tracker, buddy text)?"
- One grounding question
  - "If you could only make *one* support change before starting, what would help the most?"

## Section 4 -- The 30-Day Ritual

Each week: 1 overview page + 7 daily pages.

### Week One -- Starting the Experiment (Days 1-7)

**Weekly overview page (Page 10)**
- Focus paragraph
  - "What 4-6 lines explain that this week is about starting, using what you have, and noticing Rainbows/Unicorns?"
- Bullets
  - "This week you will..." (3 bullets: define 'enough,' use existing tools, notice setup-spending urges).
- Tiny note
  - "What sentence reassures them it's normal to feel both excited and nervous?"

**Daily pages (Pages 11-17)**
For each day, answer:
- "What is the day's theme in 3-5 words?"
- "What 3-4 lines of context set it up?"
- "What is the main writing or checklist prompt?"

Suggested themes:
- Day 1: Why now.
- Day 2: Essentials vs optional.
- Day 3: Short-term goal for this round.
- Day 4: Inventory what you already have (tools/resources).
- Day 5: Fears about 'doing it wrong.'
- Day 6: First weekend plan.
- Day 7: Week 1 reflection (what surprised you, what patterns you noticed).

### Week Two -- Meeting Your Resistance (Days 8-14)

**Weekly overview (Page 18)**
- Focus paragraph
  - "How will you describe Ambrosia + Sunshine, and that resistance is data, not failure?"
- Bullets
  - 3 examples of resistance (emotional, boredom, 'treat yourself').

**Daily pages (Pages 19-25)**
Suggested themes:
- Day 8: Recent emotional trigger and how you usually spend around it.
- Day 9: Build a non-spending comfort menu.
- Day 10: Rewrite your "I deserve this" script.
- Day 11: Boredom scroll habits and one pattern-interrupt.
- Day 12: A day that felt hard and what helped.
- Day 13: A small win in not spending for comfort.
- Day 14: Week 2 reflection (where resistance showed up, what you learned).

### Week Three -- Identity, Visibility, and Enoughness (Days 15-21)

**Weekly overview (Page 26)**
- Focus paragraph
  - "How will you lay out Stardust + Unicorns and their link to legitimacy and comparison?"
- Bullets
  - 3 ways identity spending shows up (upgrades, branding, 'real thing' gear).

**Daily pages (Pages 27-33)**
Suggested themes:
- Day 15: What 'the real thing' looks like in your mind.
- Day 16: Past purchases made to feel legit.
- Day 17: Comparison triggers (people, feeds, spaces).
- Day 18: Ways you already are that person without buying anything.
- Day 19: One visibility step using what you have.
- Day 20: Rethinking what "success" looks like this month.
- Day 21: Week 3 reflection (identity + comparison insights).

### Week Four -- Desire, Ambiguity, and What's Next (Days 22-30)

**Weekly overview (Page 34)**
- Focus paragraph
  - "How will you describe Moonbeams and the mix of patterns near the end -- fatigue, longing, 'what now'?"
- Bullets
  - 3 ways vague desire and fantasy purchases can show up.

**Daily pages (Pages 35-41)**
Suggested themes:
- Day 22: What you're longing for lately (beyond specific items).
- Day 23: Fantasy cart list (no purchasing).
- Day 24: What the challenge has revealed about what you actually want more of.
- Day 25: What you learned from any slip(s).
- Day 26: Future-you and how they handle money.
- Day 27: One habit you want to carry forward.
- Day 28: Week 4 reflection.
- Day 29: Final thoughts on this round.
- Day 30: Letter to future you / next-round you.

## Section 5 -- Integration & Your Next Cycle

### Page 42 -- What Actually Changed in 30 Days

- Prompts
  - "What feels even 5% different about money now?"
  - "Where did you surprise yourself?"
  - "What didn't change (yet), and how do you feel about that?"
- Before/after mini-check
  - A few adjectives or rating scales (e.g., anxious -> grounded, ashamed -> curious).

### Page 43 -- Your Chasing Patterns Debrief

- Checklist
  - "Which patterns did you notice the most often?" (boxes for all six).
- For top 1-2 patterns:
  - "When did this pattern show up most during the 30 days?"
  - "What seemed to help interrupt it?"
  - "Where does it still feel sticky?"

### Page 44 -- Choose Your Next Cycle

- Short intro
  - "What 3-4 lines explain that this is a practice, not a one-time fix?"
- 2-3 path sketches
  - Emotional + mood spending focus (Ambrosia/Sunshine).
  - Solutions + comparison focus (Rainbows/Unicorns).
  - Identity + desire focus (Stardust/Moonbeams).
- For each path:
  - "What might a next 30-day experiment look like?" (3 bullets each).
- Decision prompt
  - "Which path (or mix) are you choosing next, and what's one small way you'll make it easier?"

### Page 45 -- Tools, Resources, and Support

- Brief framing
  - "How will you explain that anything you offer is there to support continued experiments, not to 'fix' them?"
- Resource blocks (3-5 max)
  - For each:
    - Name.
    - What it is.
    - Which pattern(s) or phase it supports.
