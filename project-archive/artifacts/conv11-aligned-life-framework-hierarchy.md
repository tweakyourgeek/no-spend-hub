## Key Insight

The Chasing patterns are **observation tools used within** the challenge. They're not the home of the challenge. The challenge is a Money Pillar experiment that USES Chasing patterns as lenses during the curiosity/awareness phase.

---

## How the 30 Days Maps to the Lab Loop

- Week 1 (Starting) = Chasing/Curiosity phase
- Week 2 (Resistance) = Shadow phase
- Week 3 (Identity) = More Shadow
- Week 4 (Integration) = Getting + Reflection

This mapping was accidentally built into the weekly structure. The Codex is the operating system, not a curriculum customers need to learn.

---

## What This Means for the Journal

- Chasing patterns are observation tools available any day, not assigned to specific weeks
- The organizing principle is the Lab Loop, not the Chasing Series
- The customer never has to hear "experiment" - they experience a challenge
- The iterative, hypothesis-testing, data-gathering structure is for the creator to know, not customers to be taught

---

## Codex Alignment Principles Applied

- "There is nothing to fix, only information to understand" -> translated to money as "those patterns are not failures, they are protective strategies that made sense at some point"
- "A spend becomes data" -> information, not correction
- "You do not have to write in this journal to use it" / "Participation is not required" -> tools invite engagement, not compliance
- "You can't do this wrong" -> stakes stay light
- "I promise to stay in my lane" -> explicitly honors boundaries
